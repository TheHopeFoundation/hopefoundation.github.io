Artist Statement

In Dear Evan Hansen, trees play a significant role in the symbolism. Several songs reference trees as well as the plot itself. This is why we decided to make a tree. It has different colored fruit to represent how everyone is unique and different. These differences can also make people feel alone in life and as though no one understands them, similarly to the song Waving Through a Window, which the art piece is named after. The tree trunk is painted brown and the text is painted in light brown, to give it a more gentle appearance. We chose the line “Did you fall? Or did you let go?” because it shows a moment of self reflection between Evan and his internal struggles. It is the first time he really admits to himself that he tried to commit suicide. It is in this admission to himself that he is able to move forward and understand what happened to Connor, eventually revealing the truth to the Murphy's and opening himself up to his Mom. This allows him to finally get the help that he has been resisting for so long. The line on its own can simply be interpreted as a deeper inquiry into the actions of someone who has given up on something, whether by choice or not.

Artist Statement

Someone who struggles with perfectionism and people-pleasing can overexert themselves in order to fulfill the needs of others. Doing so can cause the person to feel drained and lack happiness in themselves. The drawing shows a potted plant emptying out its water to provide nutrients to the others, but in the process, its own soil becomes dry. This is symbolic of how pouring your energy into the needs of others can take way from what you need from yourself.

Artist Statement

This piece was a collaborative project between Lucy Molnar, Cole Markulis, Eric Tiller, and Patricia Turner. Instead of a canvas, the painting was done on a mannequin head to focus on the theme of mental health and mental illness. Each section was done by a different artist to include some unique perspectives and experiences surrounding mental health. Lucy chose to paint her section in black and white to demonstrate the lack of emotions felt by someone suffering from depression. She painted flower petals falling off of a flower to represent a person with failing mental health and how it might feel as though they have lost everything that once made them happy. Eric painted his section in solid gray with a red eye to represent the monotone feeling that comes when someone is struggling to feel any emotions. The eye is red to represent the exhaustion caused by stress and insomnia. He put tape over the mouth because it can be hard for people in a negative state of mine to express how they are feeling. Patty created a bright, fading design with yellow to highlight the misconception that everyone is happy unless otherwise stated and dark blue for the idea that, as you go deeper and get to know someone better, you might find that they are in a very dark place, despite pretending to be fine. Cole painted his section in black and white with messy spirals to symbolize the frustration, confusion, and stress that comes with being in a poor state of mental health.
